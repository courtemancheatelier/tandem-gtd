# The Vision Behind Tandem

Tandem exists because GTD deserves better software.

Most productivity apps borrow GTD vocabulary — "inbox," "projects," "next actions" — but skip the hard parts. They don't cascade next actions when a task completes. They don't filter by context, energy, and available time simultaneously. They don't walk you through a proper Weekly Review. And they definitely don't implement Horizons of Focus.

Tandem implements all of it. The methodology is the product.

## Why Self-Hosted?

Your GTD system is the most intimate map of your life — what you're worried about, what you're procrastinating on, what you dream about. That data belongs on your hardware, not someone else's cloud.

Self-hosted means:

- **No subscription** that can be cancelled or repriced
- **No terms of service** that can change under you
- **No vendor lock-in** — your data is in a PostgreSQL database you control
- **No telemetry** — nothing phones home, ever
- **Full control** — customize, extend, or fork as you see fit

A $5/month VPS is all you need. Or run it on your laptop. Either way, it's yours.

## Why "Tandem"?

The name works on multiple levels — partner connection (from Argentine tango), collaboration between people (twins, friends, families, small teams), and the way tasks and projects move forward together in parallel and sequence. GTD is personal, but life isn't solo.

## Core Principles

**The methodology is the product.** Every feature traces back to David Allen's framework — the five steps, the Horizons of Focus, the Weekly Review, the two-minute rule, the context/energy/time filtering. If it's not in GTD, it's not in Tandem.

**Your data is yours.** Self-hosted means self-owned. Clone the repo, start the server, own your productivity system forever.

**AI assists, never decides.** Tandem integrates AI (via MCP and in-app chat) to help you process your inbox, break down projects, and get suggestions — but it never moves tasks or changes priorities on its own. You stay in the driver's seat.

**Open source, no tiers.** Every feature is available to everyone. No "pro" plan hiding the good stuff. The license (AGPL-3.0) ensures that hosted versions must share their improvements back.

## Who Is Tandem For?

Tandem is built for people who take GTD seriously — or want to. If you've read David Allen's book and wished your tools actually supported the system, Tandem is for you.

It's also for small teams, families, and collaborators who want shared projects and delegated tasks without giving up ownership of their data.

You don't need to be technical to use Tandem, but you (or someone you trust) need to be comfortable running a small server. We're working on making that easier over time.
