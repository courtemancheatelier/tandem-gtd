# Clarify — Decide What It Means

You've captured everything into your inbox. Now comes the thinking part: for each item, you decide what it actually is and what to do about it.

## The Clarifying Questions

For every inbox item, ask yourself:

**"What is this?"**
Get specific. "Mom" might mean "call Mom about Thanksgiving" or "buy Mom's birthday gift." Vague items stay stuck.

**"Is it actionable?"**
This is the fork in the road.

### If It's NOT Actionable

Three options:

- **Trash it.** If it's no longer relevant, delete it. No guilt — things change.
- **Someday/Maybe.** It's interesting but not a commitment right now. Move it to the Someday/Maybe list and review it during your [[reflect|Weekly Review]].
- **Reference.** It's useful information you might need later. Put it in your Wiki.

### If It IS Actionable

Ask: **"What's the next physical, visible action?"**

This is the most important question in GTD. Not "what's the project?" — what's the very next thing you'd actually *do*?

- "Research health insurance" → Next action: "Google 'best health insurance plans Massachusetts 2026'"
- "Plan vacation" → Next action: "Text Sarah about which week in July works for her"
- "Fix the leaky faucet" → Next action: "Watch a YouTube video on fixing a dripping kitchen faucet"

Then apply the rules:

1. **Two-Minute Rule.** If the next action takes less than two minutes, do it right now. It's not worth the overhead of tracking it.
2. **Delegate it.** If someone else should do it, hand it off and add a Waiting For entry so you remember to follow up.
3. **Defer it.** If it'll take more than two minutes and it's yours to do, it becomes a task. If it's part of a larger outcome, it goes into a project.

## The Two-Minute Rule

This is one of GTD's most practical tools. If the next action takes less than two minutes — send the email, make the call, file the document — just do it now. The time you'd spend capturing, organizing, and reviewing it later exceeds the time to just knock it out.

Two minutes is approximate. The point is: if it's quick, don't defer it.

## Clarifying in Tandem

### Inbox Processing

Go to **Inbox** and click **Process** (or navigate to Inbox > Process). Tandem walks you through each item one at a time:

- **Create a task** — turns it into an actionable next step
- **Add to a project** — creates a task and assigns it to an existing project
- **Create a project** — if the outcome requires multiple steps
- **Move to Someday/Maybe** — parks it for later review
- **Add to Wiki** — saves it as reference material
- **Delete** — removes it entirely

The guided flow enforces the GTD clarifying logic so you don't skip steps or leave things vague.

### Tips for Processing

- **Process top to bottom.** Don't cherry-pick the easy ones. Take each item in order.
- **One at a time.** Fully process one item before moving to the next.
- **Don't put items back.** Every item leaves the inbox. It either becomes a task, a project, reference, someday/maybe, or trash. "I'll think about this later" is not an option — that's what the inbox was for.

## Next Step

Once you've clarified what something is, you need to [[organize|organize]] it — assign it a context, put it in the right project, and make sure it shows up when and where you need it.
