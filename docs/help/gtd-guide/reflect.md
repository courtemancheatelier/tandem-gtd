# Reflect — Keep It Current

You can have the best capture habits and the most organized system in the world, but if you don't review it regularly, trust erodes. And when trust goes, you go back to keeping everything in your head.

The **Weekly Review** is the habit that holds GTD together.

## The Weekly Review

David Allen calls the Weekly Review the "critical success factor" of GTD. It's a recurring appointment with yourself — ideally the same day and time each week — where you get your entire system current.

The review has three phases:

### Get Clear

Empty all your inboxes. Not just your Tandem inbox — your email inbox, your desk, your coat pockets, your phone's note app, the sticky notes on your monitor. Anything you've captured anywhere gets processed into your system.

The goal: zero inbox items. Everything has been clarified and routed to the right place.

### Get Current

Review your active lists:

- **Next Actions** — Are these still the right next steps? Cross off anything that's done. Add actions for anything that's stalled.
- **Projects** — Review every active project. Is there a clear next action for each one? Any projects that are done or no longer relevant? Any new projects to add?
- **Waiting For** — Follow up on anything overdue. Remove items that have been resolved.
- **Someday/Maybe** — Anything ready to become an active project? Anything to remove?
- **Calendar** — Review the past week (did you miss anything?) and the upcoming week (what's coming?).

### Get Creative

With your system current and your mind clear, look at the bigger picture:

- Are your projects aligned with your goals?
- Is there something important you've been avoiding?
- Any new ideas or projects to capture?
- How are your Areas of Responsibility doing?

This is where the [[horizons-of-focus|Horizons of Focus]] perspective is useful — stepping back from the runway to look at the higher altitudes.

## The Weekly Review in Tandem

Tandem has a **guided Weekly Review** that walks you through all three phases. Go to **Weekly Review** from the sidebar to start one.

The review surfaces:

- **Unprocessed inbox items** that need clarification
- **Stale projects** that haven't had activity recently
- **Overdue tasks** and upcoming due dates
- **Waiting For items** that might need follow-up
- **Someday/Maybe items** to reconsider

Tandem tracks your review streak and nudges you if a review is overdue.

## How Often?

Weekly is the standard, and for most people it's the right cadence. Some people do a lighter daily review (just scanning next actions and calendar) with a deeper weekly review.

The key is consistency. A mediocre review done every week beats a thorough review done sporadically.

## Tips

- **Block the time.** Put it on your calendar. Treat it like a meeting you can't cancel.
- **Find your spot.** A quiet place with minimal interruptions — a coffee shop, your desk after hours, a park bench.
- **Don't rush it.** A full review takes 30-60 minutes. The investment pays for itself all week.
- **Start with Get Clear.** Processing your inbox first creates momentum and clears mental space for the rest.
- **Be honest.** If a project is dead, kill it. If a someday item has been sitting there for a year, either commit to it or let it go.
