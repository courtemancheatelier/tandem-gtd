# What Is GTD?

Getting Things Done (GTD) is a personal productivity method created by David Allen. The core promise: when everything you need to do is captured in a system you trust, your mind is free to focus on actually doing the work instead of trying to remember it all.

Most people carry an invisible mental load — open loops of things they've committed to, half-remembered ideas, vague anxieties about what they might be forgetting. GTD eliminates that by giving every "thing" a place to live outside your head.

## The Five Steps

GTD is built on five steps that form a continuous cycle:

1. **[[capture|Capture — Get It Out of Your Head]]** — Collect anything that has your attention into an inbox. Don't judge, organize, or prioritize yet — just get it out of your head.

2. **[[clarify|Clarify — Decide What It Means]]** — Process each item: Is it actionable? If yes, what's the next physical action? If it takes less than two minutes, do it now. Otherwise, delegate it or defer it.

3. **[[organize|Organize — Put It Where It Belongs]]** — Route each clarified item to the right place: a project list, a next-actions list filtered by context, a waiting-for list, a someday/maybe list, or reference material.

4. **[[reflect|Reflect — Keep It Current]]** — Review your system regularly. The Weekly Review is the heartbeat of GTD — it's how you maintain trust in your system.

5. **[[engage|Engage — Do the Right Work]]** — When it's time to work, choose what to do based on your current context, available time, energy level, and priorities.

## Why It Works

GTD works because it respects how your brain actually operates:

- **Your mind is for having ideas, not holding them.** Writing things down doesn't mean you'll forget them — it means you'll stop worrying about forgetting them.
- **Decisions are easier in advance.** When you clarify "what's the next action?" during processing, you don't have to figure it out again when it's time to do the work.
- **Context matters.** You can't make a phone call when you're on the subway. Filtering by where you are and what tools you have eliminates noise and shows you only what you can actually do right now.
- **Regular reviews build trust.** If you don't trust your system, you'll go back to keeping everything in your head. The Weekly Review is what makes the whole thing work long-term.
