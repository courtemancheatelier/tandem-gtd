# Engage — Do the Right Work

This is where all the capturing, clarifying, organizing, and reflecting pays off. When it's time to work, GTD gives you a framework for choosing what to do right now.

## The Four Criteria

David Allen recommends choosing your next action based on four criteria, in this order:

### 1. Context

What can you actually do right now, given where you are and what tools you have?

If you're on the bus, you can't do @Computer tasks. If you're at the office, you probably shouldn't start @Home tasks. Context eliminates the irrelevant and shows you only what's possible.

### 2. Time Available

How much time do you have before your next commitment?

If you have 10 minutes before a meeting, don't start a 2-hour deep-work task. Filter for quick wins instead.

### 3. Energy Available

How much mental and physical energy do you have right now?

After a draining meeting, low-energy tasks (filing, organizing, routine emails) are a better fit than creative work that requires full focus.

### 4. Priority

Given what's left after the first three filters, which task has the highest payoff? This is where your judgment comes in — informed by your [[horizons-of-focus|Horizons of Focus]], your active projects, and your goals.

## Engage in Tandem

### Do Now

The **Do Now** page is Tandem's primary engagement surface. It opens with the "What Should I Do Now?" view — showing your available next actions filtered by:

- **Context** — select your current context from the filter bar
- **Energy** — filter by Low, Medium, or High
- **Time** — filter by how many minutes you have available
- **Status** — show Not Started, In Progress, or both

This directly implements Allen's four criteria. You don't scroll through a master task list hoping something jumps out — you tell Tandem your current situation and it shows you what fits.

### Quick Views

Tandem includes pre-built quick views for common situations:

- **Home Focus** — tasks you can do at home
- **Quick Wins** — short, low-effort tasks
- **Deep Work** — high-energy tasks requiring focus
- **Low Battery** — low-energy tasks for when you're drained

These are shortcuts for common filter combinations.

### The Next-Action Principle

In GTD, you never work from a project list. You work from a **next-actions list** — the collection of all the single next steps across all your projects, organized by context.

Tandem enforces this automatically. In a sequential project, only the first incomplete task appears in your Do Now list. When you complete it, the cascade engine promotes the next task. You don't have to think about which project needs attention — just look at your next actions for your current context and pick one.

## Three Types of Work

David Allen identifies three kinds of activity:

1. **Predefined work** — doing tasks on your lists
2. **Work as it shows up** — handling things in real time (interruptions, urgent requests)
3. **Defining your work** — processing your inbox, clarifying new inputs

All three are legitimate. GTD doesn't demand that you only do predefined work — it gives you the clarity to make a conscious choice about which type you're engaging in, rather than being reactive by default.

## Tips

- **Trust the system.** If your Weekly Review is current, the right tasks are on your list. Don't second-guess — pick one and start.
- **Don't overthink priority.** After filtering by context, time, and energy, the remaining options are all reasonable. Pick the one that feels right and go.
- **Mark things In Progress.** When you start a task, mark it In Progress. This gives you a visual cue of what's active and helps during your next review.
- **Capture as you go.** New ideas and tasks will come up while you're working. Capture them in the inbox and get back to what you were doing. Process them later.

## The Full Cycle

And that's GTD — a continuous loop:

1. **[[capture|Capture]]** everything
2. **[[clarify|Clarify]]** what it means
3. **[[organize|Organize]]** it in the right place
4. **[[reflect|Reflect]]** weekly to stay current
5. **Engage** — do the right work at the right time

The system works because each step supports the others. Capture without clarifying creates a junk drawer. Organizing without reflecting creates a stale system. Reflecting without engaging is just anxiety management.

When all five steps are running, your mind is clear, your commitments are tracked, and you always know what to do next.
