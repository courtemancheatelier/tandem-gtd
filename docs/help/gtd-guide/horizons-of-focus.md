# Horizons of Focus

GTD isn't just about managing tasks — it's about making sure your tasks are connected to what actually matters to you. The Horizons of Focus provide six altitudes of perspective, from the ground level of daily actions up to your life's purpose.

## The Six Horizons

Think of these as altitudes, viewed from an airplane:

### Ground Level — Next Actions

Your immediate commitments. The tasks on your Do Now list. "Call the dentist." "Send the proposal." "Buy groceries."

This is where you spend most of your time, and it's where GTD starts. But if you only work at ground level, you're being productive without necessarily being effective.

### Horizon 1 — Projects (10,000 ft)

Your active projects — outcomes you're committed to that require multiple steps. "Renovate bathroom." "Launch new product." "Plan summer trip."

Most people have 30-100 of these. Each one should have a clear outcome and at least one next action.

### Horizon 2 — Areas of Responsibility (20,000 ft)

The ongoing roles and standards you maintain in your life. These don't have finish lines — they're areas you're responsible for indefinitely.

Examples:
- **Health** — exercise, diet, medical checkups
- **Finances** — budgeting, investing, taxes
- **Career** — professional development, performance, relationships
- **Home** — maintenance, organization, improvements
- **Relationships** — family, friends, community
- **Personal development** — learning, spiritual practice, hobbies

Projects often live within areas. "Get annual physical" (project) lives under "Health" (area). Reviewing your areas helps you spot neglected parts of your life.

### Horizon 3 — Goals (30,000 ft)

What you want to achieve in the next 1-2 years. These are more specific and time-bound than areas.

Examples:
- "Pay off student loans by December"
- "Run a half marathon"
- "Get promoted to senior engineer"
- "Launch my side business"

Goals generate projects. "Launch my side business" might spawn projects like "Register LLC," "Build website," "Create first product."

### Horizon 4 — Vision (40,000 ft)

What does your life look like in 3-5 years? Where do you want to be living? What kind of work are you doing? What does your daily life feel like?

This is less about specific goals and more about the overall picture. Your vision shapes which goals you pursue.

### Horizon 5 — Purpose & Principles (50,000 ft)

Why are you here? What do you value? What principles guide your decisions?

This is the deepest level — your life's purpose, your core values, the things that don't change even when everything else does. When you're stuck on a decision at any lower level, this is where you look for answers.

## Why Horizons Matter

Without higher-altitude perspective, GTD is just efficient busywork. You can process your inbox perfectly and still spend your life on things that don't matter to you.

The horizons connect top-down:
- Your **purpose** shapes your **vision**
- Your **vision** generates **goals**
- Your **goals** create **projects**
- Your **projects** define **next actions**

And bottom-up:
- Completing **next actions** advances **projects**
- Completing **projects** achieves **goals**
- Achieving **goals** moves you toward your **vision**
- Living your **vision** fulfills your **purpose**

When these are aligned, work feels meaningful. When they're not, you feel busy but empty.

## Horizons in Tandem

Tandem implements all six horizons:

- **Ground Level / Horizon 1** — Tasks and Projects on the Do Now and Projects pages
- **Horizon 2 — Areas** — The Areas page tracks your areas of responsibility with linked projects and goals
- **Horizon 3 — Goals** — The Horizons page lets you create and track 1-2 year goals with progress indicators
- **Horizons 4-5 — Vision & Purpose** — The Horizons page includes space for vision and purpose notes that you can revisit during reviews

The **Horizons Review** (separate from the Weekly Review) is a deeper quarterly or annual exercise where you step back and look at the higher altitudes. Tandem tracks when your last horizons review was and nudges you when one is due.

## Tips

- **Start from the ground up.** Get your next actions and projects under control before worrying about vision and purpose. You can't think clearly about the big picture when small stuff is falling through the cracks.
- **Review different horizons at different cadences.** Ground and Horizon 1 get reviewed weekly. Horizons 2-3 monthly or quarterly. Horizons 4-5 annually.
- **Don't force it.** If your purpose isn't clear, that's okay. Work the lower levels and clarity often emerges naturally.
- **Look for misalignment.** If you have goals but no projects supporting them, they're just wishes. If you have projects that don't connect to any goal or area, ask why you're doing them.
