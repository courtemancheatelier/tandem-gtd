# Capture — Get It Out of Your Head

The first step of GTD is the simplest and the most important: get everything out of your head and into a trusted collection point.

## The Rule

If something has your attention — anything at all — capture it. Don't evaluate it, don't organize it, don't decide if it's important. Just write it down and move on.

This includes:

- Tasks you need to do
- Ideas you want to explore later
- Things someone mentioned that you should follow up on
- Worries, concerns, and nagging feelings
- Articles to read, movies to watch, gifts to buy
- Projects you might want to start someday
- Errands, calls, emails to send

The bar is low on purpose. If it's on your mind, it goes in the inbox. You'll sort it out later during [[clarify|clarification]].

## Why This Matters

Your brain is terrible at remembering things at the right time. It's great at reminding you about the dentist appointment — at 3am, or while you're in the shower, or in the middle of a conversation. Never when you can actually do something about it.

When you capture everything externally, two things happen:

1. **Your mind relaxes.** It stops the background loop of "don't forget, don't forget, don't forget."
2. **Nothing falls through the cracks.** Every commitment, idea, and open loop has a place to live.

## Capture in Tandem

### Inbox

The **Inbox** is your primary capture point. Head to the Inbox page and type anything — a sentence, a word, a half-formed thought. Press Enter and it's captured.

On desktop, press **Cmd+I** (or Ctrl+I) from anywhere in the app to jump straight to the inbox with the cursor ready to type.

### AI Capture via MCP

If you've connected Tandem to Claude.ai via MCP, you can capture items conversationally. Just tell Claude "add to my inbox: call the dentist about that crown" and it lands in your Tandem inbox.

### The Brain Dump

If you're new to GTD (or new to Tandem), start with a brain dump. Set a timer for 15-20 minutes and write down everything that's on your mind. Every open loop, every nagging thought, every "I should really..." — all of it goes into the inbox.

Don't stop to think about whether something is important or actionable. That comes in the next step. Right now, just get it out.

## Tips

- **Capture the thought, not the solution.** Write "figure out car insurance situation" not a detailed plan. You'll clarify later.
- **One item per entry.** "Call dentist AND pick up dry cleaning AND research vacation spots" should be three separate inbox items.
- **Don't let things pile up.** Capture throughout the day as things come up, then process during a dedicated time. An inbox that grows faster than you process it will eventually lose your trust.
